    As someone who grew up in Maine I know first hand how beautiful this state truely is. From a young age I was always encouraged to get out and be active. Here are just a few of my favorite spots that I've gotten to vist in and around the state of Maine and New Hampshire.

    Portland Headlight

    Portland Headlight; at one time a naval base, has been demilitarised and is one of the most popular tourist attractions in the greater Portland area.

    Old Port, Portland

    Portlands most popular tourist attraction, the Old Port is home to many upscale boutiques and resturants along with the port authority where cruise ships are constently coming and going during the warmer months.

    Glen Ellis Falls

    Located just off the highway, Glen Ellis Falls is a true hidden gem. In the summer it becomes quite the tourist destination, especially on warm days. If you go at the right time you can even swim into the falls (be sure to check the water temps before you go).

    Tumbledown Moutain

    Whats better than a moutian with 360 degree breath taking views? That same mountain having a lake on top! That is exactly what Tumbledown has to offer, but don't be fooled. To get this beautiful view and lake you have to climb (at some points literally) a mile and a half of rocky vertical terrain. For those with the endurance the time is definitely well spent.

    Castine Maine

    Castine Maine is not only home to Maine Maritime academy but also colonial style houses and beautiful ocean views. This picture was taken off of the Training ship state of Maine , as I'm sure you could tell from it's namesake this ship is used to train students before most go on to persue careers in the private maritime sector.
